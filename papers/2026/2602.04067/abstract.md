## 摘要

Wave breaking injects turbulence and bubbles into the upper ocean, modulating air-sea exchange of momentum, heat, gases, and sea-spray aerosols. These fluxes depend nonlinearly on sea state but remain poorly represented in coupled atmosphere-wave-ocean models, where air-entrainment velocity is often parameterized using wind speed or significant wave height alone.
